# Eugene Schwartz — Breakthrough Advertising Framework

Core principle: Copy cannot create desire. It can only channel existing desires
onto a specific product. Your job is to find the mass desire and direct it.

## The 5 Stages of Customer Awareness

### Stage 1: Most Aware
They know your product and want it. Just need a reason to act NOW.
- **Headline:** Product name + price/deal/urgency
- **Copy length:** Short — close the deal
- **Example:** "Revu Pro — 50% off this week only"

### Stage 2: Product-Aware
They know your product but aren't convinced it works for them.
- **Headline:** Lead with proof, overcome the specific objection
- **Copy length:** Medium — testimonials, case studies, results
- **Example:** "2,847 reviewers already earning. Here's what they say."

### Stage 3: Solution-Aware
They know solutions exist but haven't heard of yours.
- **Headline:** Introduce your NEW MECHANISM — the unique way you deliver the result
- **Copy length:** Medium-long — explain mechanism, differentiate, then prove
- **Example:** "The first review platform that pays for helpfulness, not hype."

### Stage 4: Problem-Aware (MOST COMMON for landing pages)
They feel the pain but haven't actively looked for solutions.
- **Headline:** Name and agitate the exact pain, then present the solution
- **Copy length:** Longer — agitate → solution → proof → close
- **Example:** "Your reviews make platforms billions. You make $0."

### Stage 5: Unaware
They don't even know they have this problem.
- **Headline:** Lead with curiosity, desire, or identity — never mention the product
- **Copy length:** Longest — educate → create awareness → present solution
- **Example:** "What if every restaurant recommendation you shared earned you money?"

## How to Identify the Stage

1. Does the audience actively search for products like yours? → Product-Aware
2. Do they know solutions exist but haven't tried yours? → Solution-Aware
3. Do they feel the pain but haven't looked for solutions? → Problem-Aware
4. Do they not realize they have this problem? → Unaware

When targeting multiple segments, write the hero for the LEAST aware segment
and address more aware segments in later sections.

## Market Sophistication

How crowded is your market?

1. **First in market:** Be simple. State the claim directly. No cleverness needed.
2. **Second in market:** Enlarge the claim. Be bigger, faster, more.
3. **Crowded market:** Introduce a NEW MECHANISM — a new way to deliver the same promise.
4. **Exhausted market:** Identity-level connection. Make them see themselves in your story.

## Key Principles for Copy

- The headline's ONLY job: make them read the second sentence
- Never sell in the headline — sell in the body copy
- Address the single biggest desire your product fulfills
- Match copy sophistication to market sophistication
- Pain motivates more than pleasure — when in doubt, agitate
- Specificity = credibility: "$4.58 per review" beats "money from reviews"
- The first 10 words do 80% of the work — spend 80% of your time on them
